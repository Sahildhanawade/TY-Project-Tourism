About Pune

Pune, the cultural capital of Maharashtra, is a dynamic blend of tradition and modernity. Known as the "Oxford of the East" due to its prestigious educational institutions, Pune has a youthful energy fueled by students, tech professionals, and artists alike. Once the stronghold of the Maratha Empire under Peshwa rule, the city now stands as a thriving hub for the IT and automotive industries, while preserving its historical landmarks like Shaniwar Wada and Aga Khan Palace.

The city is also celebrated for its vibrant arts and music scene, with numerous festivals and cultural programs held year-round. Pleasant weather, bustling markets, green parks, and a diverse food scene from street misal pav to gourmet dining make Pune a favorite destination for travelers and students.

Key Information

  • Population: 7.52 million
  • Languages: Marathi, Hindi
  • Best Time to Visit: October to February
  • Famous For: Education, history, IT industry, cultural events

Top Attractions

Hotels

Experience royal living in Jaipur's heritage hotels and modern luxury resorts. Many palaces have been converted into hotels offering an authentic Rajasthani experience.

Historic Temples

Visit the intricate Birla Mandir, Govind Dev Ji Temple, and Galtaji Temple. These sacred sites showcase magnificent architecture and spiritual significance.

Majestic Forts

Explore the iconic Amer Fort, Nahargarh Fort, and Jaigarh Fort. These historical fortresses offer panoramic views of the city and insights into Rajasthan's history.

Places

Discover iconic landmarks and cultural gems that celebrate the artistry, elegance, and vibrant heritage of the region.
